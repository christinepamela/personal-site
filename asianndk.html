Resolving Intermittent Failures in Data Acquisition Unit at Asian NDK

Electronics / Test & Measurement Failure Analysis / Troubleshooting Consultant

Synopsis

Asian NDK experienced repeated failures with a Data Acquisition Unit (DAQ), which consistently showed “OVLD” errors during production. The problem was initially classified as “No Trouble Found” (NTF), but the failures persisted in live production. I was called to perform onsite troubleshooting. Through systematic experiments comparing a known-good unit with the failing one under identical conditions, the root cause was traced to a communication fault in the mainframe of the tester.

The Challenge

Asian NDK’s DAQ unit failed repeatedly in production with OVLD errors, despite initial reporting no issues after testing. Failures created delays and uncertainty since test equipment reliability is critical for production quality control. The conflicting results between the Service Centre and production environment required onsite validation to identify the true root cause.

  • Intermittent Errors – failures occurred inconsistently (2 out of 3 times).
  • Multiple Possible Sources – the DAQ mainframe, modules, thermocouples, fixtures, software, or GPIB communication could all be potential culprits.
  • High Stakes in Production – inaccurate measurements risked both product quality and throughput.

The Solution

I conducted a controlled series of experiments onsite at NDK using one known-good DAQ mainframe and the suspect “bad” DAQ mainframe, while keeping modules, thermocouples, chambers, and fixtures constant.

  • Direct Production Tests: Good mainframe passed consistently; bad mainframe showed repeatable OVLD errors.
  • Connectivity Checks: Both units responded to IO Connection Expert and GPIB queries, reducing likelihood of card failure.
  • Bench Data Logger Software Tests: Good mainframe communicated smoothly with multiple data channels; bad mainframe failed consistently, often freezing and requiring power cycling.

Findings clearly demonstrated that the mainframe of the bad DAQ was the source of communication failure, independent of external modules or test setups.

The Results

  • Root cause identified: bad 34970A mainframe exhibited communication instability, independent of modules or external test hardware.
  • Eliminated false suspects: confirmed thermocouple, module, fixture, chamber, and GPIB interface were not contributors.
  • Clear recommendation: send the failing 34970A mainframe in for repair rather than treating it as an environmental or module-related issue.
  • Restored confidence for NDK by providing transparent, reproducible results.
  • Reduced downtime by preventing repeated return cycles under “No Trouble Found” conditions.
