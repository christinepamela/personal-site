Resolving Capacitance Measurement Failure at Panasonic

Electronics / Semiconductors Production Line Troubleshooting / Test System Recovery Consultant / Troubleshooting Lead

Synopsis

During a critical line-down situation at Panasonic, production was halted for over a week, costing the company millions daily. Despite multiple engineering teams troubleshooting without resolution, the root cause was eventually isolated to a faulty/loose connection in the test setup, not the high-frequency measurement system as initially suspected.

The Challenge

Panasonic’s production line was fully down due to capacitance measurement failures. Multiple LCR meters were swapped, yet failures persisted. Engineers suspected VXI mainframe and RF multiplexers as the culprits. Diagnostic tests showed inconsistent results across boards, slots, and fixtures, creating widespread confusion. The situation was highly tense as every day of downtime cost millions in lost production.

The Solution

I was engaged as a consultant to troubleshoot the issue systematically:

  • Verified LCR meter calibration manually to eliminate meter issues.
  • Executed controlled switching diagnostics on the VXI mainframe and RF multiplexer modules using SCPI commands.
  • Compared “good” vs “bad” boards and slots to rule out DUT vs fixture problems.
  • Isolated the fault to the fixture/test board connection rather than the measurement equipment.
  • Identified a faulty/loose cable in the setup as the true root cause of failure.

The Results

  • Root cause identified within days after weeks of downtime.
  • Production line brought back online immediately after fixing the faulty connection.
  • Millions in daily losses halted by a simple but overlooked resolution.
  • Engineering teams gained a repeatable troubleshooting methodology to avoid recurrence.
  • Highlighted the importance of fixture/test hardware integrity in high-frequency measurements.
