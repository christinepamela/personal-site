Advancing RF Competence at Scale: 3-Day PNA-X Noise Figure Measurement Training for NXP Semiconductors

Semiconductors / Electronics Corporate Training Consultant / Technical Trainer

Synopsis

NXP Semiconductors, a global leader in secure connectivity and advanced semiconductors, engaged us to deliver an intensive three-day training on PNA-X Noise Figure Measurement. The program equipped a diverse group of 40–50 engineers and technicians with the theoretical foundations, setup practices, and applied measurement skills necessary for precision RF testing.

The Challenge

Noise figure measurement is a critical step in evaluating the performance of RF devices, but accuracy depends on correct biasing, careful system setup, and consistent practices. With a wide range of participants — from technicians to senior engineers — NXP needed training that could balance foundational theory with practical skills, while ensuring relevance across varying levels of experience.

The Solution

We designed and delivered a structured three-day training program, combining lectures, demonstrations, and hands-on sessions to meet NXP’s needs:

  • Day 1: Introduction to Noise Figure Measurement
    • Fundamentals of noise in RF systems
    • Theory and application of noise figure in performance evaluation
    • Overview of PNA-X capabilities for noise figure analysis
  • Day 2: Biasing Concept & Station Setup
    • Principles of biasing for device under test (DUT)
    • Practical guidance on RF test station configuration
    • Step-by-step station setup for consistent measurement reliability
  • Day 3: Measurement Practices
    • Best practices in conducting noise figure measurements
    • Troubleshooting common sources of error
    • Real-world application exercises with the PNA-X platform

The Results

  • Enabled a large, cross-functional team of engineers and technicians to build shared competence in noise figure measurement
  • Established consistent setup and measurement practices across NXP’s RF testing operations
  • Increased confidence in troubleshooting and minimizing measurement uncertainty
  • Strengthened internal capability, reducing reliance on external support for routine and advanced testing
