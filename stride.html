Upgrading Radar Measurement Systems & Training Surveillance Engineers at STRIDE

Defense / Radar & Surveillance Systems Radar Measurement Modernization & Engineer Training Consultant / Technical Trainer

Synopsis

Over two intensive days, I led a deep dive session to refresh and enhance STRIDE’s radar measurement systems, vital for their surveillance engineering capabilities. STRIDE stands for Science & Technology Research Institute for Defence (STRIDE), under Malaysia’s Ministry of Defence. This included modernizing existing test setups and delivering focused, hands-on training to their elite team, enabling them to confidently handle both legacy equipment and future innovations.

The Challenge

  • STRIDE’s radar test infrastructure needed modernization to maintain operational readiness and accuracy across signal, frequency, and timing domains.
  • Surveillance engineers were highly skilled but required refreshers on both older and updated measurement techniques and tools.
  • Organizational vetting processes meant new ideas or equipment needed strong justification to be approved for integration.

The Solution

  • Conducted in-depth evaluation of their existing radar measurement chains—waveform generation, receiver chains, and timing accuracy.
  • Designed and delivered a 2-day training workshop that included:
    • Theory and methodology for radar measurement (e.g., pulse, Doppler, frequency accuracy).
    • Hands-on labs with both their legacy gear and potential new instruments.
    • Troubleshooting demonstrations designed to build measurable confidence in operation.
  • Framed upgrade recommendations strategically to align with their defense readiness mission, easing higher-level sign-offs.

The Results

  • Refreshed the engineering and surveillance team’s capability to maintain and innovate with radar measurement infrastructure.
  • Improved confidence in handling both legacy and modern test platforms—a force multiplier for future procurement and R&D.
  • Positioned the team to support future radar upgrades with strategic insights on test tool selection and process efficiency.
  • Built internal momentum for phased modernization, logistically sound and aligned with their mission-driven approval processes.
