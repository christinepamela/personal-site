TalentCorp – Independent Audit of Talent Service Delivery

Human Capital / Government Programs Independent Audit / Service Delivery Consultant / Auditor

Synopsis

Conducted a third-party audit of TalentCorp’s digital platforms and service delivery systems under REP and related programmes, identifying inefficiencies, stalled initiatives, and maintenance costs for legacy systems.

The Challenge

TalentCorp administers initiatives such as the Returning Expert Programme (REP)—aimed at enticing global Malaysians to return—as well as student internships and talent pipelines. However, many programmes were fully funded yet not completed, and ongoing maintenance fees were being paid for systems that no longer functioned. These issues, combined with TalentCorp's public scrutiny, raised serious concerns around financial governance and operational transparency.

The Solution

Engaged as an independent auditor, I performed a neutral, third-party evaluation of TalentCorp’s service delivery platforms. This review involved:

  • Inventorying active programmes and projects, especially REP-related systems.
  • Identifying gaps between contractual obligations and actual deliverables.
  • Assessing financial implications, especially maintenance costs for inactive or broken systems.

The Results

  • Delivered a comprehensive audit report highlighting incomplete but paid-for initiatives and ongoing costs for non-functional systems.
  • Provided transparency to stakeholders—especially regarding REP and how system inefficiencies were affecting TalentCorp’s mission.
  • While corrective actions remained with the client, the audit delivered a clear foundation for reform and accountability.
