Root Cause Analysis of Sudden Surge of Production Failure at Flextronics

Electronics Manufacturing / RF Systems Failure Analysis & Production Troubleshooting Independent Consultant / Root Cause Specialist

Synopsis

At a high-volume manufacturing site, RF power amplifier boards began failing at scale, resulting in massive line downtime. Engineers struggled to isolate the issue due to multiple potential outliers across the product, process, machinery, and testers. After repeated unsuccessful attempts, I was brought in to conduct an independent investigation. By methodically narrowing down each variable and comparing “good” and “bad” boards, the root cause was traced to a single capacitor operating out of specification. The failure analysis restored production and highlighted the importance of systematic, component-level troubleshooting.

The Challenge

Production lines for an RF Power Amplifier Board (3.3–3.4 GHz, 30 dB amplification) suffered widespread failures, halting shipments and creating urgent escalation. Symptoms included:

  • Output power drops at 3.35 GHz (delta of 1.5–2.0 dB between good and bad boards).
  • Poor flatness across frequency bands on failing boards.
  • Amplifier stage dips visible at IC19, IC14, IC7, and IC12 during signal path tracing.
  • Output filter behaving anomalously, complicating diagnosis.
  • Multiple potential culprits — test equipment, assembly process, grounding, via design, or active components — made isolating the problem extremely difficult.
  • The floor environment was tense, with teams frustrated by repeated failures and delays.

The Solution

A two-day independent review was conducted by me as an external consultant. The approach included:

  • Side-by-side comparison of good vs. bad boards across the full RF chain.
  • Stage-by-stage signal tracing at amplifier input/output nodes (IC19, IC14, IC7, IC12).
  • Component swap hypothesis: cross-verify suspect components by exchanging them between boards.
  • Filter characterization: confirm TX filter behavior to rule it out as primary cause.
  • By progressively ruling out system-level and process-level factors, the anomaly was narrowed to a capacitor mismatch at the amplifier input stage, which created power dips and non-uniform flatness.

The Results

  • Root cause identified: a capacitor operating out of spec in the input stage, causing mismatched impedance and poor gain characteristics.
  • Massive line downtime resolved in 2 days after weeks of stalled troubleshooting.
  • Provided a clear forward troubleshooting plan for Flextronics, including schematic study, component swapping, and capacitance tuning.
  • Reinforced the value of methodical, structured root-cause analysis over ad-hoc debugging.
  • Demonstrated resilience under pressure, as the consultant was the only female on the floor in a highly charged environment, yet delivered results swiftly and decisively.
