Pre-Compliance EMC Testing Solutions for Advanced Ceramics

Electronics / Advanced Materials System Integration / Lab Setup Consultant

Synopsis

Advanced Ceramics faced challenges in meeting EMC regulatory standards for their new electronic products. The company needed to establish in-house pre-compliance testing capabilities for both conducted and radiated emissions. This would allow early detection of design issues, reduce costly re-testing at external labs, and speed up product development timelines.

The Challenge

  • The company was developing electronic systems that required international compliance with EMC regulations.
  • Sending prototypes to external accredited labs was costly and time-consuming, especially when failures meant repeated redesigns.
  • They lacked an in-house setup for conducted emissions (on power lines) and radiated emissions (from enclosures and cables) measurements.

The Solution

  • Scoped out and recommended pre-compliance EMC solutions tailored to their needs.
  • Proposed a combination of spectrum analyzers, LISNs (Line Impedance Stabilization Networks), near-field probes, and semi-anechoic chamber setups for radiated emissions.
  • Delivered training to engineers on test methodology: how to identify emission peaks, compare against regulatory limits, and troubleshoot using near-field probing.
  • Created a workflow to quickly validate fixes before sending final prototypes for formal certification.

The Results

  • Advanced Ceramics successfully implemented an in-house EMC pre-compliance lab.
  • Reduced product development cycles by catching emission issues early.
  • Cut costs significantly by minimizing repeated trips to external labs.
  • Improved overall product quality and compliance confidence before certification.
