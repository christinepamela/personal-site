Improving Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) Testing for Panasonic TV QA

Consumer Electronics Test Optimization Consultant

Synopsis

Panasonic faced challenges in accurately assessing and improving the signal-to-noise ratio in their television line. Using a Noise Figure Analyzer (NFA), the project isolated performance bottlenecks in tuner and RF front-end stages, enabling a new test process that significantly improved measurement accuracy and QA reliability.

The Challenge

Panasonicâ€™s QA team identified inconsistencies in SNR performance across their TV units. Modern TVs integrate multiple RF and digital processing stages, making it difficult to pinpoint noise sources. The existing testing process lacked the precision to isolate where excess noise originated, leading to delayed troubleshooting and inconsistent product performance evaluation.

The Solution

I introduced a test methodology using a Noise Figure Analyzer (NFA) to measure noise contributions from different subsystems (primarily the RF tuner, demodulator, and IF stages). By isolating these sections and validating against reference signals, we improved the ability to locate weak points in the signal chain. The process was integrated into QA workflows, reducing ambiguity in identifying noise issues.

The Results

  • Identified root causes of poor SNR in specific tuner and front-end stages.
  • Improved precision of QA testing for TV product lines.
  • Reduced troubleshooting cycle time and increased test throughput.
  • Contributed to higher-quality product performance in production release.
