Synopsis

Conducted a market research project to analyze customer responses to two different telco offerings. The study involved controlled A/B testing across multiple consumer segments to observe behaviors and preferences, providing actionable insights for service optimization and marketing strategies.

The Challenge

  • The client sought to understand how different customer segments responded to alternative service offerings, promotions, or messaging.
  • Limited prior data made it difficult to predict adoption rates or engagement.
  • Decisions were often based on assumptions rather than observed behavior.

The Solution

  • Designed and implemented a group-based A/B testing framework.
  • Divided participants into two groups, each exposed to a different version of the service or campaign.
  • Observed, recorded, and analyzed behavioral responses, including adoption rates, engagement patterns, and customer feedback.
  • Provided comparative insights to determine which offering was more effective across segments.

The Results

  • Delivered a comprehensive report comparing behaviors of the two groups.
  • Identified preferred offerings and communication strategies for different customer segments.
  • Enabled the client to make data-driven decisions for service design, marketing campaigns, and future rollouts.
