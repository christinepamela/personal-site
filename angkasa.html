Angkasa: Building Internal Capacity for Multi-Million-Dollar Satellite Labs

Satellite / Telecommunications Infrastructure Capacity Building / Technical Training Consultant / Trainer

Synopsis

Consultation with Angkasa to train engineers in systems measurement and provide hands-on troubleshooting support, addressing gaps in technical capacity left after heavy infrastructure investments.

The Challenge

Angkasa invested heavily in satellite infrastructure, with each lab worth several million dollars in equipment and systems. However, while the technology was state-of-the-art, the company underinvested in its people. Their entire system was initially implemented by a German provider. Once the warranty period ended, responsibility shifted to local engineers who lacked the necessary skills to maintain and troubleshoot the infrastructure effectively. This created significant risk: expensive labs could underperform or fail without proper internal capacity.

The Solution

I designed and delivered a one-day training session focused on systems measurement, equipping engineers with the foundational knowledge to understand and monitor their labs. In addition, I conducted on-site troubleshooting for specific system failures, demonstrating applied methods while helping their engineers gain confidence in problem-solving. This blended approach targeted both knowledge transfer and immediate operational support.

The Results

The training and on-site consultation gave Angkasa’s engineers a stronger foundation to manage their own multi-million-dollar lab, though it wasn't enough. Instead of relying on costly external contractors, they began considering developing in-house capabilities to sustain and troubleshoot their infrastructure. The engagement highlighted the importance of balancing investment in technology with investment in people, ensuring long-term resilience of Angkasa’s satellite operations.
