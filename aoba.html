Resolving LCR Measurement Failures at AOBA Technology

Electronics / Test & Measurement Root Cause Analysis / Reliability Engineering Consultant / Technical Advisor

Synopsis

A technology manufacturer experienced repeated failures with their bias current source, which was used in conjunction with their precision LCR meter and test fixture for inductor testing. Over three years, the equipment suffered multiple fuse blowouts, disrupting measurement reliability and delaying testing workflows. Our team was engaged to conduct a detailed investigation and deliver long-term, practical engineering solutions.

The Challenge

  • Repeated fuse failures in the bias current source, interrupting workflow.
  • Inconsistent measurement reliability due to suspected fixture incompatibility with bottom-electrode inductors.
  • Lack of correlation between device characteristics, instrument operating limits, and test fixture behavior.
  • Customers required a durable solution to minimize downtime and measurement errors.

The Solution

Systematic Root Cause Analysis

  • Verified that device resistance was within equipment limits.
  • Eliminated power line and grounding issues as the primary cause.
  • Replaced damaged fuses to restore instrument functionality.

Bias Current Management

  • Identified the default bias current rail as unnecessarily high for the application.
  • Recommended configuring a lower current limit closer to expected measurement levels, preventing sudden surges and fuse damage.

Fixture Compatibility Engineering

  • Determined that the standard fixture was optimized for parallel electrode devices, creating challenges with bottom-electrode inductors.
  • Proposed two solutions:
    • Use an alternative fixture designed for bottom-electrode devices.
    • Modify the molded block of the existing fixture to ensure proper electrode contact, while retaining the option to restore the original part for service.

Cross-Site Reliability Validation

  • Recommended cross-testing with another facility, where no failures had been reported, to differentiate between systemic and site-specific issues.

The Results

  • Prevented fuse failure recurrence through the introduction of an upper-limit safeguard on bias current.
  • Delivered clear recommendations for fixture modification or replacement, ensuring reliable contact during testing.
  • Reduced downtime and avoided unnecessary servicing by stabilizing test operations.
