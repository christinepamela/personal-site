Building a Global Supply Chain for Mestrae’s Interchangeable Heels

Fashion / Logistics / Supply Chain Global Import-Export Strategy Founder & Supply Chain Architect

Synopsis

As the founder of Mestrae, I developed an end-to-end global logistics and manufacturing strategy to export our interchangeable heel products. Starting from scratch—without external consultancy—I engineered a hybrid manufacturing model, integrated supplier networks across multiple countries, and navigated complex export-import logistics to establish a resilient, scalable supply chain.

The Challenge

  • Lack of logistics experience: little knowledge of shipping, customs, or freight forwarding when international orders surged.
  • Unreliable supplier ecosystem: local manufacturing inconsistencies led to delays or inflated costs.
  • Complex supply chain needs: component sourcing from multiple countries required meticulous coordination (molds from China, uppers in Malaysia, last domestically, midsoles from Spain/Italy).
  • Landed costs and margin pressure: import duties, bank fees, and processes like Incoterms and HS codes impacted profitability, requiring detailed cost modeling.

The Solution

  • Hybrid manufacturing model: outsourced mold production to China, assembled uppers and final integration in Malaysia to support iteration and reduce MOQ risk.
  • Supplier diversification: identified trusted vendors across industries—rubber outsoles locally, leather via distributors, specialized lasts domestically.
  • Logistics deep dive: personally managed shipping, freight forwarding, and Incoterms to calculate landed cost and ensure smooth delivery.
  • Cost transparency & ROI modeling: calculated real COGS, gross margin, break-even volume, and manufacturing ROI.
  • Vertical integration & oversight: handled logistics, customs, and export documentation, often working 7 am–10 pm to maintain quality and budget control.

The Results

  • Robust global supply chain: successfully delivered Mestrae shoes to the U.S., UK, Spain, and beyond with improved reliability and lead times.
  • Control over scaling: nearly eliminated MOQ barriers while preserving product iteration speed, enabling small batch runs and rapid design changes.
  • Strategic advantage: built an operational moat by combining local craftsmanship with international sourcing, adaptable for future relaunch.
  • Accelerated learning curve: gained foundational knowledge in manufacturing, logistics, and supply chain often missing from standard business programs or consulting frameworks.
