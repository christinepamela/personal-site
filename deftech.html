Designing an EMC Testing Strategy for Defence Land Vehicles at DEFTECH

Defense / Automotive Electronics EMC Lab Strategy & Test Planning Lead Consultant / Lab Architect Malaysia

Synopsis

Consulted with DEFTECH to architect a comprehensive electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) testing laboratory and strategic testing plan for their defence land vehicles. While the facility was ultimately deployed via a third-party, the design and testing roadmap originated from this engagement.

The Challenge

DEFTECH, Malaysiaâ€™s premier developer and manufacturer of armoured and logistics vehicles, had made multi-million ringgit investments in advanced command vehicle platforms and MRO (maintenance, repair & overhaul) facilities. However, they lacked internal capacity for EMC testing which is critical for verifying resilience against electromagnetic interference in defence vehicles. With no in-house EMC lab and no established measurement strategy, they faced risks of compliance failure and extended validation timelines, potentially impacting product readiness and quality.

The Solution

As lead consultant, I developed the end-to-end strategy for an EMC laboratory tailored to defence vehicle testing, including:

  • Comprehensive design of test workflows aligned with automotive and defence EMC standards (such as ISO 11452 subsets and CISPR/IEC frameworks) to ensure vehicles withstand conducted and radiated disturbances, immunity challenges, and power injection scenarios.
  • Layout and test planning to accommodate large-scale platforms for armoured carriers, command vehicles, and hybrid systems within shielded environments.
  • Documentation of testing protocols, calibration methods, safety procedures, and lab operational structure.
  • A phased implementation roadmap suitable for hand-off to equipment integrators or labs to execute.

Technologies Used

  • EMC measurement standards (ISO 11452-xx automotive test methods, CISPR/IEC general immunity/emission test suites).
  • Shielded test facility design for vehicle-scale RF testing.
  • Test workflow development: conducted/radiated immunity, transient injection, bulk-current injection, and more.
  • Strategy documentation: SOPs, protocols, and calibration frameworks.

The Results

  • Delivered a robust, industry-aligned EMC test laboratory and procedural blueprint capable of validating land vehicle systems against interference and immunity requirements.
  • Enabled DEFTECH to move confidently toward lab deployment with a clear strategic foundation, minimizing risk when outsourcing implementation.
