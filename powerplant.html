Engineer Training for Process and Operations Optimization at Jimah Power Station

Power / Energy Process and Operations Training Consultant / Trainer

Synopsis

Conducted a one-day training session for engineers and technicians at Jimah Power Station in Port Dickson to enhance process understanding, optimize operations, and improve plant efficiency.

The Challenge

Jimah Power Station, a coal-fired GLC-operated plant, sought to enhance the technical capabilities of its operational team. Engineers and technicians required focused training to improve process optimization, minimize test and maintenance time, and ensure smooth operations under strict performance targets.

The Solution

Delivered a one-day, hands-on training session covering process optimization strategies, operational best practices, and troubleshooting techniques. The program emphasized real-world scenarios and practical methods to reduce test duration and increase yield efficiency.

The Results

  • Enhanced operational knowledge for engineers and technicians.
  • Reduced process inefficiencies, leading to minimized test and maintenance times.
  • Strengthened in-house technical capabilities, ensuring more reliable plant performance.
